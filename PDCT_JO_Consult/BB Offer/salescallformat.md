This video is a masterclass in **high-energy "POV" (Point of View) sales content**. It uses a "simulation" format that bridges the gap between reality and a demonstration of skill.

Here is a breakdown of the video format to help your agent replicate it:

---

## 1. The Hook (0:00 - 0:03)

- **Visual:** The creator is already "in character," looking slightly away from the camera as if in the middle of a workspace.
    
- **Text Overlay:** A clear, provocative headline: _"Day 1 of cold calling like [Influencer Name] in the [Industry] business."_ * **Why it works:** It uses **social proof** (referencing a known expert like Juliano Massarelli) and promises a "challenge" or "journey" (Day 1), which makes people want to see if he succeeds or fails.
    

## 2. The Interaction (0:03 - 0:55)

- **The "Double-Audio" Technique:** This is crucial. We hear both the salesperson and the "customer." It makes the scene feel authentic and high-stakes.
    
- **Active Body Language:** Notice how the creator uses his hands, gestures to the side, and maintains a "phone-call posture" even though he isn't holding a phone. This sells the realism.
    
- **The "Objection-Handling" Flow:**
    
    - **The Cold Start:** Direct and friendly.
        
    - **The Pivot:** When the customer says, "I was just looking," he immediately validates ("Just looking is just browsing's cousin") and pivots back to the product's value.
        
    - **The Time-Crunch:** When the customer resists, he offers a low-barrier-to-entry "15 minutes" to lower the stakes.
        

## 3. Visual & Technical Elements

- **Perspective:** The camera is placed at eye level, slightly to the side, making the viewer feel like a "fly on the wall" in the office.
    
- **Uniform/Context:** He is wearing his work polo and name tag. This immediately establishes authority and trust—he’s a real professional in a real environment.
    
- **Non-Verbal Cues:** He uses the "OK" sign and other hand signals to emphasize points, which keeps the visual energy high even when the dialogue is just a phone call.
    

---

## How to Adapt This for Your Agent

To use this format for a specific product and CTA, follow this template:

### Step 1: The Contextual Hook

- **Text:** "Selling [Product Name] like a pro: Day [X]" or "How to handle the [Common Objection] in [Industry]."
    
- **Product:** Ensure the agent is in their "natural habitat" (office, studio, or using the product).
    

### Step 2: The Scripted Struggle

- Have the agent "call" or "meet" a hypothetical difficult customer.
    
- **Product Placement:** The dialogue should focus on one specific feature of the product (e.g., "I saw you were looking at the 2024 Accord...").
    

### Step 3: The Close & CTA

- The video ends with a "win" (the appointment is set).
    
- **The Agent's CTA:** Instead of just saying "See you at 6," the agent can look at the camera and say, "That’s how you handle the 'just looking' excuse. Link in bio if you want my full script."
